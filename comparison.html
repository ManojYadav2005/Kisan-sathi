What is Organic vs Chemical Farming?

Organic farming uses natural fertilizers like compost and green manure, while chemical farming uses synthetic inputs such as urea and pesticides for faster growth. This page compares both for better decision-making.

Comparison Table

Aspect Organic Farming Chemical Farming
Fertilizers Compost, Green Manure Urea, DAP, NPK

Soil Health Improves Soil Structure Degrades Over Time

Benefits of Organic Farming

  • Enhances soil fertility naturally
  • Reduces pollution and promotes biodiversity
  • Produces healthier food
  • Less dependency on external chemicals
  • Supported by various government subsidies

Risks of Chemical Farming

  • Overuse leads to soil infertility
  • Pollutes nearby water bodies and environment
  • Creates pesticide-resistant pests
  • Increases input cost burden on small farmers
  • Leaves chemical residue in food
